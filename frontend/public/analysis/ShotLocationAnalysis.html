Shot Location Analysis

 

Scouting the oppositions top strikers is a very important task for managers. Most attacking players will have preferences with regard to where they like to shoot from. If the manager has detailed knowledge of the attackers tendancies and habits they can ensure that their defenders are trying their best to prevent the attackers from reaching their favourite spots.

 

We gathered data and analysed 86 different premier league goals scored by Sergio Aguero. We made a heat map of the location of the shots:

 

 

 

 
 

Despite playing as a central striker Aguero took 59.3% scored from the right hand side compared to 40.7% on the left - this reveals a tendancy that he likes to drift to the right hand side.

Aguero scored only 9.3% of the goals from outside the box – Hence if Aguero is on the ball far from the goal defenders should focus on making sure he doesn't dribble past them rather than being worried about preventing a long range shot.

 

A large proportion of the goals from inside the box were from Aguero making a well timed run in the box to get at the end of a cross.  From the goals inside the penalty area only 17% of them came from inside the 6-Yard box - This reveals a habit of Aguero to make runs outside the 6-Yard box rather than trying to get a simple tap in from a few yards out.

 

So we conclude that defenders should try to keep Aguero on the left hand side of the pitch, focus on preventing dribbles rather than long shots and to watch out for runs just outside the 6-yard box.
